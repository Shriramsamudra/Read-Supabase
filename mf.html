What are Mutual Funds?

A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment mechanism that pools capital from numerous investors. This collective corpus is then invested in a diversified portfolio of securities, such as stocks, bonds, money market instruments, and other assets, in accordance with a pre-defined investment objective. This pooling approach allows individual investors, even with small amounts of money, to gain access to a wide range of securities and sectors. The core principle is diversification, which helps mitigate risk. All mutual funds in India are regulated by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI), ensuring a fair and transparent system.

Understanding the Details

Transparency is key to trust. Here we break down the mandatory KYC process and the common charges associated with mutual funds.

The KYC Process Explained

The "Know Your Customer" (KYC) process is a mandatory identity verification step required by SEBI. It's a one-time process to ensure a secure investment environment. Understanding your KYC status is crucial. Click on a status below to learn more.

Our Charges

We believe in complete transparency with our clients. For our mutual fund distribution services, we do not levy any direct charges, fees, or costs on you, the investor.

Commission-Based Model

Our revenue comes from the commissions paid to us by the Asset Management Companies (AMCs) whose funds you invest in. This is a standard industry practice and does not affect the Net Asset Value (NAV) of your investment.

Mutual Fund Charges

All funds have operational costs, which are regulated by SEBI. The two most important are the Expense Ratio and Exit Load.

Expense Ratio (TER)

An annual fee covering operational costs, deducted from your fund's returns. A lower ratio means higher returns for you over time.

Exit Load

A fee charged if you redeem your units before a specified period. It's designed to discourage short-term trading.

Building an Ideal Portfolio

  • 1. Define Your Goals: Align investments with your time horizon. Short-term goals need stability (Debt Funds), while long-term goals can handle more risk for growth (Equity Funds).
  • 2. Assess Risk Tolerance: Understand your comfort with market fluctuations. Choose funds that match your personal capacity for risk.
  • 3. Monitor & Review: Periodically (e.g., annually) review your portfolio's performance against its benchmark and ensure it still aligns with your life goals.

Ideal fund category combinations for different risk profiles

Low Risk Profile:

Liquid Funds (for emergency) + Short Duration Debt Funds (for stability and slightly better returns).

Moderate Risk Profile:

Large Cap Index Funds (for core equity exposure) + Flexi Cap Funds (for diversification) + Short Duration Debt Funds (for balance).

High Risk Profile:

Mid Cap Funds + Small Cap Funds (for high growth potential) + Flexi Cap/Multi Cap Funds (for diversified equity) + a smaller allocation to Debt Funds for cushioning.
